Great Ormond Street Hospital professor of child health, Al Aynsley-Green, was chosen by the government and will start the £100,000-a-year job immediately.He will oversee a £2.5m annual budget and have the power to look into "any matter relating to the interests and well-being of children".Prof Aynsley-Green has also been the national clinical director for children in the Department of Health.Education Secretary Ruth Kelly said Prof Aynsley-Green would "strengthen the voice of children and young people".Prof Aynsley-Green was a lecturer at Oxford University, trained at Guy's Hospital Medical School, University of London; Oriel College, Oxford; and in Switzerland.Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland already have children's commissioners.
