A group of MPs are on a two-day fact-finding mission to Scotland to gather evidence for a report into the UK's fishing industry.MPs are deciding whether to recommend a new system of "community quotas" to conserve fish stocks.Details are contained in a Royal Commission report for the UK Government, along with the more controversial idea of closing some mixed fishing grounds completely.They will also speak to Mr Finnie, representatives of the Royal Society and the Sea Fish Industry Authority.Committee chairman Austin Mitchell said some way has to be found of harvesting mixed fisheries without wasting stocks.
