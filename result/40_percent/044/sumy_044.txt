Senior officials at the two bodies generating private income for the Queen and Prince of Wales are to be questioned by MPs.It has been reported they could be questioned about Prince Charles' spending on Camilla Parker Bowles.But BBC correspondent Peter Hunt said they are not responsible for how money is spent and may be unable to answer.The Duchy of Cornwall is a 140,000-acre estate across 25 counties, and also includes residential properties, shops, offices, stocks and shares.It was set up in 1337 by King Edward III to provide income for successive heirs to the throne.It covers the cost of the prince's public and private life - neither Charles, nor William and Harry, receive taxpayers' money from the Civil List.
