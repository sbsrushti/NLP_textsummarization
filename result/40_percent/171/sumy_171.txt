Children would be offered two hours' free sports training a week by a future Tory government, the party has said.The extra coaching would be funded by the National Lottery and would come on top of the two hours of sport a week children are supposed to get in school.Shadow sports minister Lord Moynihan said the policy would empower local clubs and create a lasting legacy.The Tories say Labour's plans to give all children two hours of sports lessons a week in schools have failed.But the Big Lottery Fund has said that complex capital projects are involved - and it was confident the money would all be allocated by next year as intended.
