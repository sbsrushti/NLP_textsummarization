Tony Blair says he will be facing the issue of trust and his own integrity head on during the election campaign.During a question and answer session with first-time voters on Five News, Mr Blair said he had no option but to "confront it" by talking to people.He also dismissed claims government plans to get 50% of young people into university would devalue degrees.In answer to a question from 22-year-old Liverpool student David Dunne about concerns over the prime minister's personal integrity, Mr Blair said talking was the only answer.Mr Blair also faced questions about British policy on Iran and its alleged nuclear weapons programme.He insisted there was no plan for an invasion of the country saying: "Sometimes people say because of what has happened in Iraq this is the next place."
