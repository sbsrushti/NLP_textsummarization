Plans to abolish 162 quangos have been unveiled by the Conservatives as part of their effort to show how government red tape can be cut.Among the targets are strategic health authorities and the new fair access regulator for universities.Tory frontbencher John Redwood said Britain needed a slimmer government and lower taxes to be competitive.The Tories say the strategic health authorities are not needed as it is better that local people, rather than officials, run hospitals and surgeries."Far from improving public services, spending taxpayers' money on quangos has led only to more bureaucrats, more regulation and higher taxes."His party leader, Michael Howard, argued a change in direction was needed to get a grip on spending.The government has announced plans to cut 100,000 civil servants as part of its efficiency drive.The Liberal Democrats have said they would cut the number of Whitehall departments to make sure money reaches frontline services.
